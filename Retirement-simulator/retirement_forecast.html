Key Decisions By Retirement Phase

Every stage of your retirement timeline carries specific strategic priorities. Below is your sequential blueprint map.

30 → 65

Preparation Years

These are your active working years. Your focus should rest entirely on maximizing accumulation channels and asset location sequencing.

Key Decisions

  • Retirement timeline margins
  • Portfolio structural locations
  • Traditional vs. Roth contributions
Why this matters: Large Traditional deferrals create massive silent future tax liabilities.
65 → 65

Retirement Bridge Years

The period between stepping away from work and the beginning of Medicare. Managing your visible income bracket is critical.

Key Decisions

  • Roth conversion windows
  • ACA healthcare subsidy bridging
  • Geographic arbitrage adjustments
Why this matters: Like Tom, choosing to leave your assets unoptimized risks millions in structural leakage.
Age 65 → Age 73

Medicare Years

Healthcare decisions become structural. Managing modified income limits avoids high premium surcharges.

Key Decisions

  • Medicare premium matching (IRMAA)
  • Social Security filing timelines
  • Income bracket stabilization
Why this matters: Missing key subsidy brackets triggers major healthcare surcharges.
Age 73+

RMD Years

Forced asset liquidations begin. Tax management becomes critical as the government mandates minimum withdrawals.

Key Decisions

  • Required minimum distributions (RMDs)
  • Withdrawal sequencing order
  • Strategic legacy transfers
Why this matters: RMD spikes can trigger unexpected taxation on Social Security and Medicare.
Age 80+

Legacy / Care Years

The final chapters. Focus shifts to estate efficiency, medical planning, and family proximity support models.

Key Decisions

  • Long-term care funding
  • Family proximity logistics
  • Generation transition vehicles
Why this matters: Early structuring prevents estate capital erosion during transition.
