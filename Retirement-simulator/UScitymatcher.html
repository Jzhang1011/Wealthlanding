U.S. Retirement City Decision Tree

Find the right U.S. city for your retirement phase.

This matcher separates two very different decisions: where to live during U.S.-based Roth conversion years, and where to settle after the conversion window is done. It screens tax status, filing status, Social Security, passive income, senior supports, healthcare depth, cost, climate, and airport access.

How it works Matcher Results Comparison table Sources
Logic driven

Start with the retirement phase, not the state.

A no-income-tax state can be excellent during Roth conversion years, but after the conversion window is complete, healthcare depth, property tax, insurance, senior transportation, and long-term care may matter more.

Benefit screening

You don't need to know local benefit limits.

The matcher asks for filing status and recurring income, then classifies income-tested senior supports as likely useful, maybe useful, or unlikely to apply. Exact program eligibility still requires county/state verification.

Sources and planning notes

  1. No-broad-based-income-tax states and retirement-income tax treatment should be verified each year; several states with no state income tax or exempt retirement income can still have meaningful property, sales, insurance, or local taxes.
  2. Tennessee property tax relief is described by the state as a program for low-income elderly and disabled homeowners, disabled veteran homeowners, and surviving spouses; therefore this page treats it as income-sensitive rather than universal.
  3. Florida property tax exemptions are administered locally by county property appraisers; the Florida Department of Revenue notes further benefits for senior citizens and other groups, but local rules and income limits must be checked.
  4. Texas homestead exemptions reduce appraised value for a primary residence, and Texas provides over-65/disabled protections such as school-tax ceilings in many contexts; property tax burden still varies heavily by county, city, school district, and home value.

Disclaimer: This page is an educational prototype, not tax, legal, Medicare, Medicaid, real-estate, or investment advice. State and county rules change. Senior benefits may depend on age, disability, homeownership, residence duration, income, assets, veteran status, and local ordinances. Verify with official state/county agencies and qualified advisors before relocating or making Roth conversion decisions.
